Research report

Recent ArXiv papers on scale-up networking design

A compact report covering recent papers on collective communication, photonic interconnect reconfiguration, hierarchical GPU communication, and network-aware scheduling.

TL;DR

  • Communication is becoming more application-aware, not just flow-aware.
  • Hierarchy matters: NVLink, RDMA, and scale-up fabrics should be treated differently.
  • Programmable collective-control layers are becoming a serious systems direction.
  • Adaptive / reconfigurable photonic fabrics look promising when reconfiguration costs are modeled honestly.
2026-03-18 · LLM serving

Multi-stage Flow Scheduling for LLM Serving

Proposes a stage-aware network scheduler for disaggregated LLM serving. The key idea is that scheduling should understand dependencies among KV retrieval, collectives, and transfers, instead of treating each flow independently.

ArXiv link

2026-03-13 · MoE / NCCL

NCCL EP: Towards a Unified Expert Parallel Communication API for NCCL

Introduces a unified MoE communication layer on top of NCCL Device API, with low-latency and high-throughput modes spanning NVLink and RDMA. Strongly relevant to hierarchical scale-up communication design.

ArXiv link

2026-03-12 · Collective control

NCCLbpf: Verified, Composable Policy Execution for GPU Collective Communication

Adds a verified eBPF-style policy execution layer for NCCL plugins, pointing toward safe and programmable collective-control planes.

ArXiv link

2026-02-11 · Photonic interconnects

To Reconfigure or Not to Reconfigure

Studies when topology reconfiguration is worth it for all-to-all communication in circuit-switched photonic interconnects. One of the most directly relevant papers for future scale-up fabric design.

ArXiv link

2026-02-09 · Scale-up domains

Harvest: Adaptive Photonic Switching Schedules for Collective Communication in Scale-up Domains

Synthesizes topology reconfiguration schedules for collectives in photonic scale-up domains, balancing reconfiguration cost against congestion and propagation delay.

ArXiv link

My take

The strongest design signal across these papers is co-design: topology, runtime scheduling, and collective behavior are no longer separable. The most relevant reads for scale-up fabric design are Harvest, To Reconfigure or Not to Reconfigure, and NCCL EP.

Reading order

  1. Harvest
  2. To Reconfigure or Not to Reconfigure
  3. NCCL EP
  4. NCCLbpf
  5. Multi-stage Flow Scheduling for LLM Serving
